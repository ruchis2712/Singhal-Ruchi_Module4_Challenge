### Q1. How is the district summary affected?

	(i) Since Thomas High School 9th graders form 1% of the district population, removing math and reading scores for them drops the passing math%, passing reading% and overall passing% for the district by 1%

	(ii) The average math and reading scores for the district remain unaffected as these students are not considered in calculating the mean values


### Q2. How is the school summary affected?

	(i) Only Thomas High School is affected by deleting the scores of 9th graders for Thomas High School, and replacing them with Nan

	(ii) For Thomas High School, the % passing scores (reading, math and overall) are affected but the averages are not affected for the same reason provided in question#1


### Q3. How does removing the ninth graders’ math and reading scores affect Thomas High School’s performance, relative to the other schools?

	(i) It significantly drops the % passing for reading, math and overall

	(ii) The average reading and math scores remain unchanged

### Q4. How does removing the ninth-grade scores affect the Math and Reading Scores by Grade, Scores by School Spending, Scores by School Size, and Scores by School Type? 

	(i) In each of these analysis (i.e. by Grade, school spending, size and school type), only the bins where Thomas High School belonged i.e. 9th Grade, $630-644, Medium (1000-2000), and Charter Type are affected

	(ii) Within each of these bins, the average scores remained the same but the % passing was affected. The magnitude of affect was based on how much Thomas High School student count contributed to the overall student count in each of the bins
